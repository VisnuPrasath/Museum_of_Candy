Milk candy is a type of confectionery that is made using milk as a primary ingredient. It typically involves simmering or heating milk with sugar and other flavorings until it reaches a certain consistency, and then allowing it to cool and solidify into a candy-like form. Milk candy can vary in texture, from soft and chewy to hard and brittle, depending on the specific recipe and cooking process.
